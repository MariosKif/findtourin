## Top 10 Destinations You Should Visit in 2026

### 1. Greece

Greece remains essential in 2026 thanks to new direct flights, improved island infrastructure, and a culinary renaissance in Athens. Visit May through June or September through October for warm weather without peak crowds.

Beyond Santorini, explore the Peloponnese for ancient theaters and wineries, or head to the Zagori region for [hiking tours](/tours?category=Hiking+%26+Trekking) through dramatic gorges. Recent [UNESCO World Heritage](https://whc.unesco.org/) nominations renewed global attention on the Greek islands. [Explore tours in Greece](/tours?country=Greece) to discover experiences beyond the postcards.

### 2. Japan

Japan blends ultra-modern cities with ancient tradition, earning a firm place among the top destinations for 2026. New shinkansen routes open remote regions, and a favorable exchange rate keeps costs manageable.

Autumn delivers brilliant foliage with fewer tourists than cherry blossom season. Walk the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage trails for [adventure experiences](/tours?category=Adventure), or enjoy a kaiseki dinner through a [food and wine tour](/tours?category=Food+%26+Wine). [Discover tours in Japan](/tours?country=Japan) for guided access to hidden temples and local markets.

### 3. Portugal

Portugal delivers world-class experiences at prices lower than Spain or France. Lisbon's pastel neighborhoods, Porto's ribeira district, and the Algarve coastline rival the best of the Mediterranean.

Tour Porto's port wine cellars on a [cultural tour](/tours?category=Cultural), or explore the Douro Valley by boat. The Algarve offers over 300 days of sunshine per year, making it a top [beach and island](/tours?category=Beach+%26+Island) escape. Portugal's tourism board reports a [30% increase in sustainable tourism](https://www.visitportugal.com/) since 2024.

### 4. New Zealand

New Zealand packs glaciers, fiords, volcanic plateaus, and beaches into two islands. December through February brings warm weather, while March through April offers autumn colors with fewer crowds.

The Milford Track is a four-day trek through Fiordland that books out months ahead. Queenstown delivers every adrenaline activity imaginable. The Tongariro Alpine Crossing offers single-day volcanic terrain that feels like another planet. This is a dream for [nature tour](/tours?category=Nature) enthusiasts.

### 5. Morocco

Morocco overwhelms the senses with spice market colors, tagine aromas, and Sahara silence under star-filled skies. March through May and September through November bring comfortable temperatures.

Take a guided [cultural experience](/tours?category=Cultural) through Fez's medieval medina, or book a multi-day camel trek into the Erg Chebbi dunes. The [World Travel & Tourism Council](https://wttc.org/) ranks Morocco among Africa's fastest-growing tourism economies for 2026.

### 6. Iceland

Iceland's volcanic landscape of geysers, glaciers, and hot springs feels genuinely otherworldly. June through August offers midnight sun and the best [hiking and trekking](/tours?category=Hiking+%26+Trekking) conditions. September through March is prime Northern Lights season.

Explore the Snaefellsnes Peninsula for glaciers and lava fields in a single day. The Westfjords remain one of Europe's last true wilderness areas, delivering extraordinary [nature experiences](/tours?category=Nature).

### 7. Peru

Peru contains the Amazon's greatest biodiversity, the Andes' most iconic archaeological site, and South America's best food scene. Leading travel guides named Peru a top destination for cultural immersion in 2026. The dry season from May through October is ideal for trekking.

The Inca Trail to Machu Picchu sells out months ahead, but alternatives like the Salkantay trek offer equally stunning scenery. Lima's Miraflores district is a [food and wine](/tours?category=Food+%26+Wine) destination in its own right.

### 8. Croatia, Thailand, and Costa Rica

Croatia's Dalmatian Coast, Dubrovnik's old town, and the Plitvice Lakes make it one of Europe's most photogenic countries. Visit May through June or September to skip summer crowds.

Thailand's diversity spans megacity Bangkok, misty northern mountains, and tropical southern islands. November through February is the most comfortable season. Chiang Mai serves as the base for elephant sanctuaries and cooking classes.

Costa Rica contains nearly six percent of the world's biodiversity. Walk suspended bridges through Monteverde Cloud Forest or hike around Arenal Volcano. [National Geographic](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/travel/destination/costa-rica) calls Corcovado the most biologically intense place on Earth.
