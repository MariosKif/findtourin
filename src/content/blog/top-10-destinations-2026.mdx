## Our Top Picks: Best Travel Destinations 2026

### 1. Greece

Greece remains an essential destination in 2026 thanks to new direct flight routes, improved island infrastructure, and a culinary renaissance in Athens. Visit from May to June or September to October for warm weather without peak-season crowds.

Beyond Santorini sunsets, explore the Peloponnese for ancient theaters and wineries, or head to the Zagori region for Europe's best [hiking and trekking](/tours?category=Hiking+%26+Trekking) through dramatic gorges. [Explore tours in Greece](/tours?country=Greece) to go beyond the postcards.

### 2. Japan

Japan's seamless blend of ultra-modern cities and ancient tradition makes it one of the most compelling places to visit in 2026. New shinkansen routes open remote regions, and a favorable exchange rate makes it more accessible than ever.

Cherry blossom season draws crowds, but autumn delivers brilliant colors with fewer tourists. Walk the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage trails for [adventure](/tours?category=Adventure), or dive into Japanese cuisine with a kaiseki dinner or ramen-making class through a [food and wine tour](/tours?category=Food+%26+Wine). [Discover tours in Japan](/tours?country=Japan).

### 3. Portugal

Portugal delivers world-class experiences at prices impossible in neighboring Spain or France. Lisbon's pastel neighborhoods, Porto's ribeira district, and the Algarve coastline rival anything in the Mediterranean.

April through June and September through October offer the best weather. Tour Porto's historic port wine cellars on a [cultural tour](/tours?category=Cultural), or explore the Douro Valley's terraced vineyards by boat. The Algarve is a reliable [beach and island](/tours?category=Beach+%26+Island) escape with over 300 days of sunshine per year.

### 4. New Zealand

New Zealand packs glaciers, fiords, volcanic plateaus, and pristine beaches into two islands, making it a dream for [nature lovers](/tours?category=Nature). Visit December through February for warm weather, or March through April for autumn colors and fewer crowds.

The Milford Track is a four-day trek through Fiordland that books out months ahead. Queenstown delivers every adrenaline activity imaginable, while the Tongariro Alpine Crossing offers a single-day hike through volcanic terrain that looks like another planet.

### 5. Morocco

Morocco overwhelms your senses in the best way: spice market colors, tagine aromas, and Sahara silence under star-filled skies. Visit March through May or September through November for comfortable temperatures.

Marrakech's Jemaa el-Fnaa square transforms into an open-air food festival each evening. Take a guided [cultural experience](/tours?category=Cultural) through Fez's medieval medina, or book a multi-day camel trek into the Erg Chebbi dunes for a once-in-a-lifetime [adventure](/tours?category=Adventure).

### 6. Iceland

Iceland's volcanic landscape of geysers, glaciers, and hot springs feels genuinely otherworldly. June through August offers midnight sun and the best [hiking and trekking](/tours?category=Hiking+%26+Trekking) conditions, while September through March is prime Northern Lights season.

Explore the Snaefellsnes Peninsula for glaciers and lava fields in a single day, or snorkel between tectonic plates in the Silfra fissure. The Westfjords remain one of Europe's last true wilderness areas, offering extraordinary [nature experiences](/tours?category=Nature).

### 7. Peru

Peru contains the Amazon's greatest biodiversity, the Andes' most iconic archaeological site, and South America's best food scene. The dry season from May through October is ideal for trekking and wildlife spotting.

The Inca Trail to Machu Picchu sells out months ahead, but alternatives like the Salkantay trek offer equally stunning scenery. Lima's Miraflores district is a [food and wine](/tours?category=Food+%26+Wine) destination in its own right, home to several of the world's top-ranked restaurants.

### 8. Croatia

Croatia rivals Italy and Greece in beauty while remaining more affordable. The Dalmatian Coast's crystal-clear waters, Dubrovnik's old town, and the ethereal Plitvice Lakes make it one of Europe's most photogenic countries.

Visit May through June or September to avoid summer crowds. Island hop along the coast by ferry for one of Europe's great [beach and island](/tours?category=Beach+%26+Island) experiences, hitting Hvar, Korcula, and Vis along the way.

### 9. Thailand

Thailand's extraordinary diversity spans megacity Bangkok, misty northern mountains, and tropical southern islands. November through February is the cool, dry season and the most comfortable time to visit.

Bangkok's street food scene ranges from Michelin-starred noodle stalls to after-dark barbecue joints. Chiang Mai is the base for elephant sanctuaries and hands-on cooking classes, while the southern islands offer world-class diving and pristine [beach escapes](/tours?category=Beach+%26+Island).

### 10. Costa Rica

Costa Rica contains nearly six percent of the world's biodiversity in a country smaller than West Virginia. The dry season from December through April is most popular, but the green season offers lush landscapes, fewer tourists, and lower prices.

Walk suspended bridges through Monteverde Cloud Forest, hike around Arenal Volcano, or explore Corcovado National Park on the Osa Peninsula, described by National Geographic as the most biologically intense place on earth.

## Frequently Asked Questions About the Best Travel Destinations for 2026

### What are the best travel destinations 2026 for first-time international travelers?

Portugal, Thailand, and Costa Rica are excellent choices for first-time international travelers. All three have well-developed tourism infrastructure, are easy to navigate, offer great value, and are welcoming to visitors. [Browse beginner-friendly tours](/tours) to find guided experiences that handle all the logistics.

### When is the cheapest time to visit these destinations?

Shoulder seasons consistently offer the best value. For European destinations like Greece, Portugal, and Croatia, that means May to June and September to October. For Southeast Asia, June and October provide deals with manageable weather. Booking tours in advance also helps lock in better prices.

### Are guided tours worth it, or should I travel independently?

Guided tours are especially valuable in destinations with complex logistics, language barriers, or limited infrastructure, such as Peru, Morocco, and Iceland. They save planning time, provide cultural context from local experts, and often include skip-the-line access. For more guidance, see our article on [how to choose a tour agency](/blog/how-to-choose-tour-agency).

### How can I make my trip more sustainable?

Choose tour operators with verified sustainability practices, stay in locally owned accommodation, eat at local restaurants, and minimize single-use plastics.
