## Top Regions and Best Bulgaria Tours by Type

### Sofia and the Mountains: Capital Plus Rila Monastery

Sofia is Bulgaria's capital and the standard arrival point. The city itself is a 2-day visit, with the Alexander Nevsky Cathedral, the Boyana Church frescoes, and the National Archaeology Museum as the headline stops. Most Bulgaria tours pair Sofia with [Rila Monastery](https://www.findtoursin.com/attractions/bulgaria/sofia/rila-monastery), the country's most important cultural site, 120 km south in the Rila Mountains.

Day trips from Sofia to Rila are the standard format. For travellers with more time, the [Seven Rila Lakes hike](https://www.findtoursin.com/tours/category/hiking-trekking/in/bulgaria) adds a 6-hour mountain walk through alpine cirque lakes at 2,200 m. The trail starts a 30-minute drive from Rila Monastery and is the most-photographed natural landscape in Bulgaria.

### Plovdiv: Continuous Habitation Since 6,000 BC

Plovdiv is one of Europe's oldest continuously inhabited cities, with a 6,000-year archaeological record. The Roman Theatre is still in use for summer concerts. The Old Town is a maze of National Revival mansions, painted facades, and small museums. The [Plovdiv heritage walk](https://www.findtoursin.com/tours/in/bulgaria) is a 3 to 4 hour tour covering the Old Town, the Roman Forum, and the Kapana arts district.

The best Bulgaria tours include Plovdiv as a 1 to 2 day stop between Sofia and Veliko Tarnovo. The European Capital of Culture 2019 designation drove substantial restoration of the historic core, so the city is more accessible than it was a decade ago.

### Veliko Tarnovo: Medieval Capital and Tsarevets Fortress

Veliko Tarnovo was Bulgaria's medieval capital from the 12th to 14th centuries. The Tsarevets Fortress (the [Tsarevets attraction](https://www.findtoursin.com/tours/in/bulgaria) reconstructed citadel) sits on a peninsular rock loop in the Yantra River. The Sound and Light show on Tsarevets, held on summer evenings, is the most-cited Bulgarian travel experience. The Old Town's Samovodska Charshia bazaar is a working artisan street with rope-makers, coppersmiths, and traditional Bulgarian sweet shops.

### Black Sea Coast: Beach Days and Ancient Towns

The Bulgarian Black Sea coast runs from Varna in the north to Sozopol and Burgas in the south. The best beach destinations are Sozopol's Old Town (a UNESCO-protected peninsula of wooden Bulgarian Revival houses) and the smaller villages of Sinemorets and Tyulenovo for quieter sand. Burgas's Strandzha National Park offers coastal hiking. Browse [beach and island tours](https://www.findtoursin.com/tours?category=Beach+%26+Island) for coastal options.

### Bulgarian Wine and Rose Valley

Bulgaria is one of the world's oldest wine-producing countries (Thracian wine production dates to 6,000 BC) and the [Bulgarian wine route](https://www.findtoursin.com/tours/category/food-wine/in/bulgaria) covers the Melnik, Plovdiv, and Black Sea wine regions. Rose Valley between Karlovo and Kazanlak produces 70 percent of the world's rose oil. May to early June is rose-harvest season; the rose-picking experience is the country's signature agritourism format.
