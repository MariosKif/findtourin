## Top Regions and Best Albania Tours by Type

### Albanian Riviera: Ksamil, Saranda, Borsh

The Albanian Riviera runs along the Ionian coast from Vlore in the north to Ksamil in the south, opposite Corfu. [Ksamil and Saranda](https://www.findtoursin.com/tours/in/albania/ksamil) are the headline beach destinations: Ksamil for turquoise water and small islands you can swim to, Saranda for the working seaside town with restaurants and ferry connections to Corfu.

The best Albania tours of the Riviera run as 3 to 4 day stays with a beach-day pace, plus a Butrint archaeological visit and an optional day trip across to Corfu. The region is at full operator capacity June through September; April-May and October offer the best balance of weather and crowd levels.

### Berat: City of a Thousand Windows

[Berat](https://www.findtoursin.com/tours?country=Albania) is a UNESCO-protected Ottoman-era town in central Albania, named for the white-and-grey rows of windows in the Mangalem and Gorica quarters that line the Osum River. The hilltop castle is still inhabited, with families living inside the medieval walls. The [Onufri Museum](https://www.findtoursin.com/tours/category/cultural/in/albania) holds 16th-century Albanian Byzantine icons. A 1 to 2 day Berat visit fits between a Tirana arrival and a Riviera leg.

### Butrint: Layered Ancient Mediterranean

Butrint is Albania's most-visited archaeological site, an Adriatic peninsula 18 km south of Saranda with Greek, Roman, Byzantine, Venetian, and Ottoman ruins layered in a single national park. The 4th-century BC theatre and the 5th-century AD baptistery (with one of Europe's most important early-Christian mosaic floors) are the headline assets. Most Riviera-based travellers visit Butrint as a half-day morning trip.

### Albanian Alps: Theth and Valbona

The Albanian Alps in the north (called the Accursed Mountains in local lore) deliver alpine hiking that rivals the Slovenian Julian Alps at lower cost. [Theth National Park](https://www.findtoursin.com/tours/in/albania) is the headline trailhead, with the 17 km Theth-to-Valbona pass as the country's signature day hike. Most travellers visit Theth as a 2 to 3 day extension after the coast or a Tirana base.

The hiking season runs June through October. Earlier months can have lingering snow on higher passes; later months close mountain huts and ferry services on Lake Komani (the alpine reservoir that links Berisha to the Valbona valley by morning ferry).

### Tirana: The Modern Capital

Tirana has transformed in the last decade from grey-block post-Communist capital to one of the Balkans' most colourful urban scenes, with painted apartment blocks, the renovated Skanderbeg Square, and a strong cafe culture. The Bunk'Art museums (former Communist nuclear bunkers converted to history exhibitions) are the most-cited urban attraction. A 1 to 2 day Tirana stop bookends most Albania tours.
