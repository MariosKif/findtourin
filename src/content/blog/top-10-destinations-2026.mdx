## 1. Greece: Where Ancient History Meets Island Paradise

Greece is not just a destination; it is an experience that lingers in your memory long after you return home. From the whitewashed villages of the Cyclades to the forested gorges of Crete, the country offers a staggering variety of landscapes packed into a relatively compact geography. In 2026, Greece continues to dominate traveler wish lists thanks to improved infrastructure on lesser-known islands, new direct flight routes from major cities, and a culinary renaissance that is putting Athens on par with Europe's great food capitals.

### Best Time to Visit

The sweet spot for visiting Greece is May through June or September through October. During these shoulder months, you will enjoy warm weather perfect for swimming, significantly fewer crowds than the July-August peak, and lower accommodation prices. Spring is particularly magical on the mainland, when wildflowers carpet the hillsides and archaeological sites are pleasantly uncrowded. If you are heading to the islands, late September still delivers beach-worthy temperatures with the bonus of harvest festivals and freshly pressed olive oil.

### What to Do

Beyond the iconic Santorini sunsets and Parthenon visits, consider exploring the Peloponnese peninsula for its ancient theaters, Venetian fortresses, and world-class wineries. The Zagori region in northwestern Greece offers some of Europe's best [hiking and trekking](/tours?category=Hiking+%26+Trekking) through dramatic gorges and over centuries-old stone bridges. For island hoppers, skip the heavily touristed routes and explore the Dodecanese or northeastern Aegean islands for authentic village life. ## 2. Japan: A Year-Round Masterpiece of Culture and Nature

Japan has cemented its place as one of the world's most compelling travel destinations, and 2026 brings even more reasons to visit. The country's seamless blend of ultra-modern technology and deeply rooted tradition creates a travel experience unlike anywhere else on earth. New shinkansen routes are making remote regions more accessible, and a favorable exchange rate is making this once-expensive destination more approachable for international visitors.

### Best Time to Visit

Cherry blossom season from late March through mid-April draws the biggest crowds, but Japan rewards visitors in every season. Autumn, from mid-October through November, paints the countryside in brilliant reds and golds, with far fewer tourists than spring. Winter is ideal for powder skiing in Hokkaido or soaking in outdoor onsen surrounded by snow. Summer brings vibrant matsuri festivals throughout the country, though the heat and humidity in cities like Tokyo and Osaka can be intense.

### What to Do

Tokyo alone could fill a two-week itinerary, from the Tsukiji Outer Market's fresh sushi breakfasts to the serene Meiji Shrine nestled in an urban forest. Kyoto's seventeen UNESCO World Heritage Sites deserve several days of exploration, ideally with a knowledgeable local guide. For [adventure seekers](/tours?category=Adventure), the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage trails through the Kii Peninsula offer multi-day hiking through ancient cedar forests and past sacred shrines. Do not miss the [food and wine scene](/tours?category=Food+%26+Wine) either. Japanese cuisine was named a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage, and experiencing an intimate kaiseki dinner or learning to make ramen from scratch are highlights no traveler forgets. ## 3. Portugal: Europe's Best-Value Destination Gets Even Better

Portugal has quietly become one of Europe's most beloved destinations, and for good reason. The country delivers world-class experiences at prices that would be impossible in neighboring Spain or France. Lisbon's pastel-colored neighborhoods cascade down hillsides to the Tagus River, Porto's ribeira district feels like stepping into a painting, and the Algarve coastline rivals anything in the Mediterranean.

### Best Time to Visit

April through June and September through October offer the ideal combination of pleasant weather, manageable crowds, and reasonable prices. The Algarve enjoys over 300 days of sunshine per year, making it a reliable [beach and island](/tours?category=Beach+%26+Island) escape even in the shoulder months. Lisbon and Porto are comfortable for walking year-round, though summer temperatures in the interior Alentejo region can exceed 40 degrees Celsius.

### What to Do

Lisbon's Alfama district is a labyrinth of narrow streets, fado music venues, and tiny restaurants serving grilled sardines. Take the iconic Tram 28 for an atmospheric tour of the city's hilliest neighborhoods. In Porto, a [cultural tour](/tours?category=Cultural) of the historic port wine cellars in Vila Nova de Gaia is essential, followed by a stroll across the Ponte Dom Luis I for panoramic views. Beyond the cities, the Douro Valley offers Portugal's answer to wine country, with terraced vineyards lining a winding river that you can explore by boat, car, or on foot.

## 4. New Zealand: The Adventure Capital of the Southern Hemisphere

New Zealand packs an almost absurd amount of natural beauty into two relatively small islands. Glaciers, fiords, volcanic plateaus, subtropical forests, and pristine beaches exist within hours of each other, making it a dream destination for [nature lovers](/tours?category=Nature) and adrenaline junkies alike. In 2026, improved visitor infrastructure and new eco-lodges are making it easier than ever to explore responsibly.

### Best Time to Visit

The New Zealand summer from December through February offers the warmest weather and longest days, perfect for hiking and water activities. However, March and April deliver stunning autumn colors and fewer crowds, particularly on the South Island. For skiing, July through September is prime time in Queenstown and Wanaka. The shoulder months of October and November bring spring wildflowers and newborn lambs dotting green hillsides.

### What to Do

The Milford Track, often called the finest walk in the world, is a four-day [hiking and trekking](/tours?category=Hiking+%26+Trekking) experience through Fiordland National Park that books out months in advance. Queenstown delivers every adventure activity imaginable, from bungee jumping and jet boating to paragliding above Lake Wakatipu. On the North Island, the Tongariro Alpine Crossing is a single-day hike through volcanic terrain that looks like another planet. Do not overlook the cultural dimension either. Maori cultural experiences offer profound insight into New Zealand's indigenous heritage and its deep connection to the land.

## 5. Morocco: A Feast for Every Sense

Morocco is a country that overwhelms you in the best possible way. The colors of the spice markets, the call to prayer echoing across ancient medinas, the scent of tagine simmering in a riad courtyard, and the silence of the Sahara at night under a canopy of stars create a sensory richness that few destinations can match.

### Best Time to Visit

March through May and September through November are the most comfortable months for exploring Morocco. Summer temperatures in Marrakech regularly exceed 40 degrees Celsius, making sightseeing punishing. Winter is mild in coastal cities like Essaouira but can be cold in the Atlas Mountains. Ramadan dates shift each year, so check the calendar before planning, as some restaurants and attractions may have reduced hours during this period.

### What to Do

Marrakech's Jemaa el-Fnaa square transforms from a daytime market into an open-air food festival each evening, and the surrounding souks are a treasure trove of handcrafted goods. A guided [cultural experience](/tours?category=Cultural) through Fez's medieval medina, the largest car-free urban area in the world, reveals centuries of artisan traditions still practiced today. For [adventure](/tours?category=Adventure), a multi-day camel trek into the Erg Chebbi dunes provides a once-in-a-lifetime Sahara camping experience. The blue-washed city of Chefchaouen in the Rif Mountains and the coastal town of Essaouira add variety to any itinerary.

## 6. Iceland: Earth's Most Dramatic Landscape

Iceland has transformed from a niche destination into a global travel phenomenon, and its appeal shows no signs of fading. The island sits atop the Mid-Atlantic Ridge, where the Eurasian and North American tectonic plates pull apart, creating a landscape of active volcanoes, geysers, glaciers, and hot springs that feels genuinely otherworldly.

### Best Time to Visit

June through August offers midnight sun, accessible highland roads, and the best weather for [hiking and trekking](/tours?category=Hiking+%26+Trekking). September through March is Northern Lights season, with the darkest months of November through January offering the best aurora viewing conditions. Winter driving requires caution and a sturdy four-wheel-drive vehicle, but rewards brave travelers with ice caves, frozen waterfalls, and a level of solitude impossible in summer.

### What to Do

The Golden Circle route covering Thingvellir National Park, Geysir, and Gullfoss waterfall is the classic introduction, but Iceland's true magic lies along the less-traveled paths. The Snaefellsnes Peninsula packs glaciers, lava fields, sea cliffs, and fishing villages into a single day's drive. The Westfjords remain one of Europe's last true wilderness areas. Snorkeling between tectonic plates in the Silfra fissure and exploring ice caves inside Vatnajokull glacier are [nature experiences](/tours?category=Nature) you simply cannot have anywhere else on the planet.

## 7. Peru: From Inca Citadels to Amazonian Wilderness

Peru is a country of superlatives. It contains the Amazon basin's greatest biodiversity, the Andes' most iconic archaeological site, and South America's most celebrated food scene, all within a single border. In 2026, new sustainable tourism initiatives are improving the visitor experience while protecting fragile ecosystems and supporting local communities.

### Best Time to Visit

The dry season from May through October is ideal for trekking to Machu Picchu and exploring the highlands. Lima's coastal climate is pleasant year-round, though a persistent grey mist called garua blankets the city from June through November. The Amazon basin is hot and humid throughout the year, but the dry season from May through October means lower water levels, better wildlife spotting, and fewer mosquitoes.

### What to Do

The four-day Inca Trail to Machu Picchu is the most famous trek in South America, but permits are limited and sell out months ahead. Alternatives like the Salkantay and Lares treks offer equally stunning scenery with more availability. Lima's Miraflores district is a [food and wine](/tours?category=Food+%26+Wine) destination in its own right, home to several of the world's top-ranked restaurants. A multi-day Amazon lodge stay from Puerto Maldonado or Iquitos brings you face to face with pink river dolphins, giant otters, macaws, and an ecosystem of staggering complexity. ## 8. Croatia: The Mediterranean's Rising Star

Croatia has emerged from the shadow of its more established Mediterranean neighbors to become a destination that rivals Italy and Greece in beauty, while remaining more affordable and less congested. The Dalmatian Coast's crystal-clear waters, Dubrovnik's terracotta-roofed old town, and the ethereal Plitvice Lakes have made it one of Europe's most photogenic countries.

### Best Time to Visit

May through June and September are the golden months for Croatia. July and August bring scorching heat and cruise ship crowds that can make Dubrovnik's narrow streets feel claustrophobic. Spring is ideal for Plitvice Lakes, when snowmelt feeds the waterfalls to their most impressive flow. The shoulder season also brings lower prices for accommodation along the coast, where summer rates can be surprisingly steep.

### What to Do

Dubrovnik's city walls offer a two-kilometer walk with views over terracotta rooftops and the shimmering Adriatic. Island hopping along the Dalmatian Coast by ferry is one of Europe's great [beach and island](/tours?category=Beach+%26+Island) experiences, with Hvar, Korcula, and Vis each offering distinct character. Plitvice Lakes National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, features sixteen terraced lakes connected by waterfalls that flow through dense forest. Split's Diocletian's Palace is a living monument where Roman ruins house modern cafes, shops, and homes in a seamless blend of ancient and contemporary life.

## 9. Thailand: The Gateway to Southeast Asia

Thailand has been welcoming travelers for decades, yet it continues to surprise and delight even repeat visitors. The country's extraordinary diversity, from megacity Bangkok to misty northern mountains to tropical southern islands, means you could visit ten times and have ten completely different experiences. Excellent value for money, world-famous hospitality, and one of the planet's greatest culinary traditions seal the deal.

### Best Time to Visit

November through February is the cool, dry season and the most comfortable time to visit most of Thailand. March through May is brutally hot, particularly in Bangkok and the central plains. The monsoon season from June through October brings rain, but often in short, dramatic bursts rather than all-day downpours. The shoulder months of June and October can offer excellent deals with manageable weather, especially on the Gulf Coast islands like Koh Samui and Koh Phangan.

### What to Do

Bangkok demands at least three days. The Grand Palace and Wat Pho are essential, but the real magic is in the city's street food scene, from Michelin-starred noodle stalls in Chinatown to the after-dark barbecue joints of Sukhumvit. Chiang Mai in the north is the base for elephant sanctuaries, hill tribe villages, and some of the best [food and wine experiences](/tours?category=Food+%26+Wine) in Asia, including hands-on cooking classes. The southern islands offer everything from full-moon parties on Koh Phangan to serene diving around the Similan Islands. For a less-traveled [adventure](/tours?category=Adventure), Khao Sok National Park's ancient rainforest and limestone karsts are among the most spectacular natural landscapes in Southeast Asia.

## 10. Costa Rica: The Eco-Tourism Pioneer

Costa Rica has been at the forefront of sustainable tourism for decades, and in 2026 the country continues to set the standard for responsible travel. Despite being smaller than West Virginia, it contains nearly six percent of the world's biodiversity, spread across twelve distinct ecological zones. A well-developed tourism infrastructure makes it easy to explore safely, while a genuine national commitment to conservation means your visit directly supports environmental protection.

### Best Time to Visit

The dry season from mid-December through April is the most popular time, with sunny skies and lower humidity. However, the green season from May through November offers lush landscapes, fewer tourists, lower prices, and excellent wildlife viewing as migratory species arrive. The Caribbean coast has its own microclimate, with the driest months being September and October. Regardless of when you visit, pack layers as temperatures can vary dramatically between the coast and cloud forest elevations.

### What to Do

Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve is a misty, moss-draped wonderland where you can walk suspended bridges through the canopy and spot resplendent quetzals. Arenal Volcano provides a dramatic backdrop for [hiking and trekking](/tours?category=Hiking+%26+Trekking), hot spring soaking, and white-water rafting on the Pacuare River. On the Pacific coast, Manuel Antonio National Park combines accessible wildlife viewing with some of the country's best [beach and island](/tours?category=Beach+%26+Island) scenery. The Osa Peninsula's Corcovado National Park, described by National Geographic as the most biologically intense place on earth, is the ultimate [nature](/tours?category=Nature) destination for those willing to venture off the beaten path.
