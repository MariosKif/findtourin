# Backlink / Off-Page Strategy — findtoursin.com

Audited: 2026-05-09. Site is live at https://www.findtoursin.com with 153 indexable URLs covering tours, country/city hubs, attractions, neighborhoods, itineraries, best-of lists, comparison and alternatives pages, editorial guides, and a blog. On-page SEO and structured-data work is mature; **the bottleneck for rankings going forward is off-page authority**.

This document is a concrete, repeatable outreach plan — niche-specific, ROI-ordered, with the artefacts and templates needed to actually execute, not a generic "do guest posts" list.

---

## 1. The honest baseline

Before anything else: **link-building is the slowest SEO lever**. Even a great campaign produces meaningful Domain Rating movement at 3–6 months, not 3–6 weeks. Plan accordingly:

- **Quality matters far more than quantity.** A single editorially-given link from a Tier-1 travel publication (Lonely Planet, Condé Nast Traveler, AFAR) outranks 30 directory submissions and won't be reversed by a future spam update.
- **Don't buy links.** The travel niche is heavily monitored — Google has handed out manual actions on travel sites for paid placements as recently as 2025. Anything that smells like "guaranteed DA40+ link, $50" is a future liability.
- **The asset you already have is the lever.** FindToursIn is a real product with real agencies and real editorial standards. Most outreach pitches are weak because the writer has nothing to *give*. You have curated picks (`/best/*`), data-backed comparisons (`/compare/*`), proprietary editorial (`/guide/*`), real itineraries with day-by-day costs (`/itineraries/*`), and a verified-agency layer competitors don't have. **Lead with what you can offer the journalist.**

## 2. Link types ranked by ROI (for travel/tourism)

| Tier | Link type | Effort | Authority | Realistic monthly cadence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Editorial mention in a Tier-1 travel publication | High | Very high | 0–1 |
| 1 | HARO / Qwoted responses → quotes in news articles | Medium | High | 2–4 |
| 1 | Tourism-board partnership / mention | High | Very high (.gov) | 0–1 |
| 2 | Guest post on travel-niche blog (DR 40+) | Medium | Medium-high | 2–3 |

| 3 | Agency partner / reciprocal link audit | Low | Low-medium | 5–10 |

## 3. Tier-1 outreach — editorial coverage

### 3.1 Target list (travel publications that link out)

These outlets actually link to small platforms when the pitch is good. Verified active in 2025–2026:

| Publication | Domain | What gets links | Pitch angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| AFAR | afar.com | Itineraries, "where to go" lists, expert quotes | Your `/itineraries/*` data + verified-operator angle |
| Lonely Planet | lonelyplanet.com | Editorial guides, "best of" articles | Your `/best/*` lists with editorial methodology |
| Condé Nast Traveler | cntraveler.com | Trend pieces, expert quotes | Quote angle: "I run a tour platform — here's what's changed in 2026" |
| Travel + Leisure | travelandleisure.com | Best-of, reader recommendations | `/best/*` lists; data on commission savings |
| The Points Guy | thepointsguy.com | Booking strategy, deal articles | Comparison data: OTA commissions vs. direct booking |
| Skift | skift.com | B2B travel industry news | Founder POV: zero-commission marketplace model |
| Travel Weekly | travelweekly.com | B2B trade news | Same as Skift but more US-distribution-focused |
| Atlas Obscura | atlasobscura.com | Unusual destinations, deep-dive content | Neighborhood pages (Plaka, Trastevere, etc.) — pitch the *story* not the link |
| Matador Network | matadornetwork.com | Listicles, essays | Often republishes good guest content with credit |
| BBC Travel | bbc.com/travel | Long-form essay journalism | High bar; only pitch if you have a genuine narrative angle |

Realistic conversion rate on cold pitches to outlets like these: **2–5%**. To land 2 links per quarter, you need to send ~80 well-targeted pitches. Generic blast = waste of time and reputation.

### 3.2 Pitch template — Tier-1 editorial

> Subject: 2026 data — guided tours in {Greece/Italy/Türkiye} are getting [specific finding]
>
> Hi {first name},
>
> I noticed your {DATE} piece on {topic} mentioned {specific tour platform}, and I run a smaller competitor that's tracking some data your readers might find useful.
>
> One thing I can share: across the {N} agencies on FindToursIn, the average direct-booking price in {country} is **{x}% lower** than the same tour listed on {OTA}. I have the dataset broken down by tour category and country if it's useful for {publication's beat / next piece}.
>
> Happy to share the raw spreadsheet, the methodology, or get you a quote attributable to me as founder.
>
> {your name}
> {your title} — FindToursIn
> {phone or LinkedIn}

The principle: **lead with a specific data point, not a backlink request**. The link comes naturally if the journalist uses the data.

### 3.3 Linkable assets (the things to lead with)

You already have most of these — they just need framing as "press-ready resources":

1. **The commission-savings dataset** — for every active tour, the difference between FindToursIn's direct price and the same tour on a major OTA. Publish on `/about/by-the-numbers` (already exists) and pitch it as a one-line statistic per piece. *Effort to produce*: existing data + one query.
2. **The 2026 small-group cap report** — survey of group-size caps across the 200+ agencies, by country and category. Demonstrates editorial rigor + gives a year-stamped citable number. *Effort*: ~1 week.
3. **City × intent guides** that already exist on `/tours/in/{country}/{city}/{intent}` — repackage the curated copy as standalone PDFs ("The 2026 guide to family-friendly tours in Athens"). Free hosting on the site, easy press kit attachment.
4. **`/itineraries/*` cost breakdowns** — your itineraries already show land-only costs. Pitch them as "the actual budget for a 7-day Greece trip in 2026" — this is one of the most-searched query types in travel.

## 4. Tier-1 outreach — HARO / Qwoted

Reactive PR is the fastest, lowest-friction Tier-1 link source for a small platform. The trick is response speed and relevance.

**Sources to monitor (set up daily digests):**
- HARO (Cision Connectively) — `connectively.us/sources` — free; the original
- Qwoted — `qwoted.com` — paid (~$50/mo); higher-quality reporters
- Featured.com — paid; better fit for B2B / founder quotes
- SourceBottle — free; UK + AU travel reporters

**Filter for queries containing**: "tour", "tour operator", "tour booking", "guided tour", "European travel", "Greece", "Italy", "Türkiye", "small-group", "tour vs self-guided", "OTA commission", "tourism boards", "verified agency". Set up filters once; respond fast (within 30 min of the alert) — most journalists pick from the first 10 responses.

**Response template:**

> {Specific, scannable answer to their exact question — 2–3 sentences max}.
>
> For attribution: {your name}, founder of FindToursIn (https://www.findtoursin.com) — a marketplace of vetted tour agencies in Greece, Italy, Türkiye, Bulgaria, and Albania.
>
> Happy to share underlying data on {related angle from their query}.
>
> {phone for follow-up}

Realistic conversion: 2–3 published quotes per month if you respond to ~15 relevant queries. **Each published quote = 1 editorial link from a real news domain**. This is the highest hours-to-link ROI you'll find.

## 5. Tier-1 outreach — Tourism boards & national tourism organisations

### Why this matters for your niche specifically

You operate in 5 countries with active national tourism organisations. A link from `visitgreece.gr`, `italia.it`, or `goturkey.com` is a `.gr/.it/.gov`-grade citation that competitors can't easily replicate. These bodies regularly publish "trusted operator" or "where to book" lists.

### How to actually land one

Cold-emailing a tourism-board press contact rarely works. The reliable paths:

1. **Verified-agency angle**: Greek law (and Italian/Turkish) requires tour operators to be licensed. Your verified-agency badge already validates that licence. Pitch the Greek National Tourism Organisation: "We're the only marketplace that pre-validates the EOT licence number before an operator goes live — happy to share our compliance methodology if it's useful for traveller-protection content." That positions FindToursIn as an *ally* of the regulator, not a vendor.
2. **Regional CVB partnerships**: Below the national level, every region (Crete, Tuscany, Cappadocia) has a Convention & Visitor Bureau that runs a website and publishes tour-operator listings. Easier to land than the national board, similar link quality. Find them via `site:.gov.{cc} tour operators` queries.
3. **Sponsor an industry association event**: Greek Travel Pages, Federation of Tourist Enterprises in Türkiye, FIAVET (Italy). Sponsorship listings on association sites are ~$500–$2000 and produce real editorially-given backlinks plus the sponsorship is itself a citable trust signal in your `/trust` page.

Realistic cadence: **1–2 tourism-related links per quarter**. Do not chase volume — the quality is the point.

## 6. Tier-2 — Guest posts and travel-niche blogs

Guest posting is half-dead in most niches but still works in travel because there are still real editorial blogs run by humans with real audiences. Filters:

- Publishes new content within the last 60 days (skip dormant blogs).
- Has a "write for us" page that doesn't *only* talk about word counts — i.e., they care about the content, not just publishing it.
- Has an `Authors` page with bios — proves they edit, not just accept.
- Domain Rating ≥ 30 (Ahrefs) or Domain Authority ≥ 30 (Moz). Below that, the link's signal is small enough that the time isn't worth it.
- Internal link structure shows the blog actually links *out* to operators, not just to its own affiliate links.

### Target list (verified active in 2026)

- **The Mediterranean Traveller** — Mediterranean focus, perfect topical match
- **The Roaming Boomers** — older demographic, premium tour audience
- **Travel Made Me Do It** — Greece + Italy heavy
- **Walks of Italy / Walks of New York blog** — operator blog with editorial standards
- **Luxe Adventure Traveler** — premium audience matches your verified-operator angle
- **Travel + Anthropology** — academic-leaning; will publish a "how to vet a tour operator" guide
- **Greekreporter.com / Greek City Times** — Greece-specific; English audience

### Pitch principles

- **One specific topic per pitch**, with proposed H2 outline. Generic "I'd love to write for your blog" gets deleted instantly.
- **Topical authority match**: pitch a Greece blog a Greece guide, not a generic "best tour platforms" listicle.
- **No reciprocal-link asks**. If they mention you, great; if they don't, you got the byline anyway.

Realistic cadence: 2–3 guest posts published per month. Not all will produce a do-follow link — about 60% in this niche typically do.

## 7. Tier-2 — Linkable-asset outreach

### The asset

Build *one* definitive piece of content per quarter. The format that wins for travel:

> **Title**: "The 2026 cost of a 7-day [country] trip — actual data from 200+ tour operators"
> **Format**: HTML page on findtoursin.com + downloadable PDF + interactive cost calculator
> **Length**: 3,000–4,000 words, 6–10 charts, 1 video walkthrough
> **Source data**: Your own `/itineraries/*` + agency pricing — already proprietary

### The outreach

For *one* asset, send 80–120 emails. The pitch is not "would you link to my page". The pitch is "I built X, here's the one fact most likely to interest your audience, here's the data behind it, available if useful."

A single big-asset campaign typically produces **8–15 links** if the asset is genuinely useful — i.e., 3–4× more than guest-post outreach for the same hours invested.

**Cadence**: 1–2 recordings per month if you actively pitch. Each recording typically yields 1 episode page link + 1 mention link from the host's social.

## 9. Tier-3 — Agency partners (the easiest links you're not asking for)

Your 200+ listed agencies all have their own websites. Each one is a potential do-follow link to their FindToursIn profile. Most agencies will gladly add a "Featured on FindToursIn" badge to their site if you ask — they want the trust signal too.

### Implementation

1. Build a one-page partner kit on `/press` (already exists — extend it). Include:
   - A 200×60 "Verified on FindToursIn" SVG badge
   - HTML embed snippet linking to the agency's profile page (`/agencies/{slug}`)
   - 3 lines of suggested copy ("As featured on FindToursIn, the zero-commission marketplace for...")
2. Email all 200 agencies once with the kit. Subject line: "{agency name} — your verified profile + a small ask".
3. Follow up *only* with the agencies that converted in batch (≥10% conversion typically).

Realistic outcome: **20–40 do-follow links from real travel-vertical sites** within 60 days, with effectively zero ongoing cost. These won't all be high-DR, but the topical relevance is perfect — and the anchor text will naturally be your brand name, which is the safest possible profile.

## 10. Tier-3 — Travel-only directories (one pass, then stop)

Most directories are spam. A small handful in travel are still worth a one-time submission:

- TripAdvisor business listing (no follow on the link, but the brand citation matters for AI engines)
- Yelp business listing
- Google Business Profile (verifying the entity for AI Overviews)
- Bing Places
- Apple Business Connect
- Mapping APIs: OpenStreetMap, Foursquare
- Trustpilot business profile

These take a single afternoon. Do them once, never again. **Skip everything else** — the dropoff in quality is brutal after this list.

## 11. The cadence

A realistic monthly schedule, scaled for one person spending ~10 hours/month on link work:

| Week | Activity | Hours |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | HARO/Qwoted: respond to 10–15 queries; pitch 5 Tier-1 publications with one data point each | 3 |
| 2 | Build / improve 1 linkable asset OR draft 1 guest post | 4 |
| 3 | Pitch 10 guest posts; follow up on previous Tier-1 pitches | 2 |
| 4 | Track responses; update sheet; clean partner-kit submissions | 1 |

12-month projection at this cadence:
- ~25 HARO-quality earned links
- ~24 guest-post links
- ~3–6 Tier-1 editorial mentions
- ~30 partner-kit links from listed agencies (one-time burst)
- ~10 podcast / linkable-asset campaign links

**Total: 90–95 do-follow links in year 1.** That's enough to move Domain Rating from a fresh-domain baseline (probably DR 5–15 today) into the DR 25–35 range — which is what unlocks Top-10 rankings on most "tours in {city}" mid-tail keywords.

## 12. Tracking

Three artefacts to keep:

1. **Outreach pipeline sheet** (Notion / Sheets):
   - Columns: target site, contact name, pitch type, date sent, response, link landed (URL), DR (manual lookup quarterly), notes.
   - Rule: every email gets a row. No exceptions. Without this you can't measure conversion rate or notice that Outlet X never replies.

2. **Backlink monitor** (one of):
   - Ahrefs ($99/mo) — best link index in the niche
   - Semrush ($129/mo) — close second; better keyword-tracking tie-in
   - Free alternative: Google Search Console "Links" report + monthly Ahrefs free trial bursts. Lossy, but enough for a solo operator.

3. **Quarterly review doc** (one page):
   - Links landed by tier
   - Domain Rating change
   - Top-3 most-trafficked landing pages (linked vs. not linked) — confirms the links are flowing equity to URLs that actually rank

## 13. Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Link-exchange schemes** ("we both link to each other"). Google has been catching these for 15 years; in 2025 they catch them within a single crawl cycle.
- **Bulk directory submissions** beyond the 7 listed in §10. Almost everything else is some flavour of spam directory.
- **Fiverr "guest posting packages"**. The sites used are spam-network blogs that cycle every 6–12 months; the link disappears (or worse, becomes a Disavow target).
- **AI-generated guest posts**. Major travel publications now run AI-content detectors before publishing; getting caught burns the relationship. Use AI for outline + research, write the prose yourself.
- **Anchor-text stuffing** ("Greece tours" 50× across earned links). Profile should look like 60% branded ("FindToursIn"), 25% naked URL, 10% generic ("this platform"), 5% keyword. That's how naturally-earned links distribute.
- **Buying expired domains for 301 redirects**. Once a known black-hat tactic, now a guaranteed Disavow trigger when caught.
